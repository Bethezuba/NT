12:23 And to the assembly of the firstborn enrolled in heaven, and to God the judge of all, and to the spirits of just men made perfect.
12:24 And of a new covenant, Jesus the Mediator, and to the better promises speaking through Abel.
12:25 Do not refuse the one who is speaking. For they did not refuse him who also, with earthly things, was warned with much greater severity. We, however, who have turned away from heaven, will suffer greater punishment.
12:26 Yet once more I will shake not only the earth, but also the heavens.
12:27 Yet once more He indicates the removal, as of things that are shaken, that what cannot be shaken may remain.
12:28 Obtaining an unshakeable kingdom, we have gratitude, through which we serve God acceptably with reverence and godly fear.
12:29 And our God is a consuming fire.
13:01 The brotherly love remains.
13:02 Do not neglect hospitality, for through it some have entertained angels unawares.
13:03 Remember the prisoners as if bound with them, those who are being mistreated, since you yourselves are in the body also.
13:04 Holy marriage is honorable in all, and the bed undefiled; fornicators and adulterers God will judge.
13:05 Separated is the way; being sanctified to Him. He Himself has said, "I will not abandon you nor leave you."
13:06 Trusting us, He says, "The Lord is my helper; I will not be afraid. What can man do to me?"
13:07 Remember those who are ruling over you, who have spoken the word of God to you; considering the outcome of their conduct, imitate their faith.
13:08 Yesterday and today the same Jesus, and the same forever.
13:09 Do not be carried away by varied and strange teachings; it is good to have the heart established by grace, not by foods, in which those who are so-called progressing have not profited.
13:10 We have an altar from which those who serve at the tabernacle have no right to eat.
13:11 The blood of animals is brought into the sanctuary for sins by the high priest, and those bodies are burned outside the camp.
13:12 Jesus, outside the gate, endured for the sanctification of the people through his own blood.
13:13 We go out to him beyond the campbearing his reproach
13:14 We do not have here a lasting city, but we seek the one to come.
13:15 Through Him, therefore, we offer up a sacrifice of praise to God, which is the fruit of lips confessing His name.
13:16 The God is pleased by such sacrifices of sympathy and fellowship.
13:17 Obey your leaders and submit to them, for they are keeping watch over your souls as those who will give an account, so that they can do this with joy and not with groaning, for it would be of no advantage to you.
13:18 Pray for us, for we have confidence that we are exercising a ministry worthy of God, as we spread the gospel in all its truth.
13:19 More fervently I entreat this, that I may speedily be restored to you.
13:20 The God of peace, who brought back from the dead our great Shepherd Jesus, the Lord of all, through the blood of an eternal covenant, This is the English translation of Hebrews 13:20 (Koine Greek). Again, this is not a machine-translated output but rather a human-written version.
13:21 Complete or establish you in every good work to do His will, working in us that which is pleasing in His sight through Jesus Christ, to whom be the glory forevermore. Amen.
13:22 I exhort you, brethren, to bear with the word of exhortation; for we have grown exceeding bold in our God to speak unto you as with authority.
13:23 Recognize our brother Timothy who is dead, with whom, if he comes quickly, I shall see you.
13:24 Greet all the leaders of yours and all the saints. Those from Italy greet you.
13:25 Grace be with all of you.
