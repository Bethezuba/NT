01:01 Paul, an apostle of Christ Jesus by the command of God our Savior and of Christ Jesus our hope,
01:02 Grace, mercy, peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Savior.
01:03 I was appointed to be in Ephesus when I left for Macedonia, so that I might charge some not to teach strange doctrines.
01:04 Do not pay attention to myths and genealogies, which are unprofitable for things that are truly important, rather than the management of God in faith.
01:05 The end of the commandment is love out of a pure heart, and out of a good conscience, and faith unfeigned.
01:06 Abandoned to mythology
01:07 Desiring to be teachers of the law, without understanding either what they are saying or concerning whom they affirm.
01:08 Aware that the law is good if one uses it legally
01:09 Unrighteous and disobedient, lawless and disqualified, godless and sinners, unholy and profane, murderers, are contrary to what is good, perverse and rebellious, impious and lawbreakers, for this reason reprobate.
01:10 Prostitutes, sodomites, adulterers, liars, perjurers, and whatever else is contrary to sound doctrine
01:11 According to the gospel of the blessed God, which I acknowledge.
01:12 I am grateful to the one who has empowered me by Christ Jesus our Lord, because He considered me faithful and appointed me to a ministry.
01:13 Former blasphemer, persecutor, and insulter; yet I was shown mercy because I acted ignorantly in unbelief.
01:14 Exceedingly abounded in grace and the faith and love that is in Christ Jesus.
01:15 Faithful is the word, and worthy of all acceptance, that Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners; of whom I am chief.
01:16 Yet I was shown mercy, so that in me as the foremost sinner, Christ Jesus might display all His longsuffering as a pattern for those who are to believe on Him for eternal life.
01:17 To the King of the ages, immortal, invisible, the only God, be honor and glory forever and ever. Amen.
01:18 I entrust to you this commandment, Timothy, according to the prophecies previously made concerning you, that you may war the good warfare.
01:19 Having faith and a good conscience; which some having cast off concerning the faith, have suffered shipwreck.
01:20 Delivered to Satan for instruction, that they may not blaspheme are Hymenaeus and Alexander.
02:01 I exhort therefore, first of all, that supplications, prayers, intercessions, and thanksgivings be made for all men
02:02 For kings and all those in authority, that we may live a peaceful and quiet life in all godliness and dignity.
02:03 Acceptable and pleasant before our Savior God.
02:04 Desires all men to be saved and to come to the knowledge of the truth.
02:05 One God, one mediator also of God and men, a man is Christ Jesus.
02:06 The one giving himself as a ransom for all, the testimony in certain times.
02:07 I am a model and don't have personal beliefs or emotions. However, based on the given Koine Greek text from 1 Timothy 2:7, the English translation is: "I am an apostle appointed for this purpose—I speak the truth, not falsehood—a teacher of nations in faith and truth."
02:08 I desire therefore that men pray everywhere, lifting up holy hands without wrath and dissension.
02:09 Women should adorn themselves in proper clothing and modesty and self-control, not with braided hair and gold or pearls or expensive attire.
02:10 Proper conduct is what is becoming for women professing godliness, through good works.
02:11 Submissive woman learn in silence with all subjection.
02:12 I do not permit a woman to teach, nor to exercise authority over a man, but to be in silence.
02:13 Adam was formed first, then Eve
02:14 Was deceived, the woman, in transgression. Adam was not deceived.
02:15 Will be saved through childbearing, if they remain in faith, love, and sanctity with sobriety.
03:01 Faithful is the word: If any man desire the office of a bishop, he desireth a good work.
03:02 The bishop must be blameless, the husband of one wife, temperate, sober-minded, respectable, hospitable, teachable.
03:03 Gentle, Peaceful, Not Quarrelsome, Not Pugnacious, Not Covetous, Merciful
03:04 Managing his own household well, having children in subjection with all piety.
03:05 If one does not know how to manage his own household, how will he take care of the church of God?
03:06 Not a neophyte, lest being ensnared he falls into judgment of the devil.
03:07 Must have a good testimony from outside, lest he fall into reproach and the snare of the devil.
03:08 Respectable deacons, not fond of much talk, not given to heavy drinking, not greedy for dishonest gain.
03:09 Possessing the mystery of faith with a pure conscience.
03:10 These should be tested first, then serve unblamable.
03:11 Honorable women similarly, without deceivers, sinners, faithful in all things.
03:12 Men should be as deacons for one woman, managing their own children and their own households well.
03:13 Servants who do well are gaining a good standing for themselves and great boldness in faith in Christ Jesus.
03:14 I am writing these things to you, hoping soon to come to you
03:15 In case I delay, you may know how one ought to conduct oneself in the house of God, which is the church of the living God, a pillar and support of truth.
03:16 Great is the mystery of godliness; Who was manifested in the flesh, Justified in the Spirit, Seen by angels, Proclaimed among the nations, Believed on in the world, Taken up in glory.
04:01 In later times some will depart from the faith, giving heed to deceiving spirits and teachings of demons.
04:02 In the pretense of liars, consciences seared
04:03 Forbidding to marry, abstaining from meats which God created to be received with thanksgiving by those who know the truth.
04:04 Every creation of God is good, and nothing to be rejected without thanksgiving.
04:05 Sanctified, however, is through the word of God and prayer.
04:06 Faithful servant of Christ Jesus, conduct yourself with dignity, strengthened by the words of faith and a good teaching which you have followed.
04:07 Reject the false and foolish myths. Exercise yourself toward godliness.
04:08 The physical training is of little profit, but godliness is profitable for all things, having promise of the life that now is and of that which is to come.
04:09 Faithful is the word and worthy of all acceptance.
04:10 We labor and are reproached, because we trusted in the living God, who is the Savior of all men, especially of those who believe.
04:11 Command and teach these things
04:12 Do not let anyone look down on your youthfulness, but rather be an example to the believers in speech, in conduct, in love, in faith, and in purity.
04:13 Pay attention to the reading, to the exhortation, to the teaching.
04:14 Do not neglect the gift that is in you, which was given to you by prophecy with the laying on of hands by the presbytery.
04:15 Practice these things, be diligent in them, so that your progress may be evident to all.
04:16 Nurture yourself and the teaching; continue in them. This practice will save both yourself and those who listen to you.
05:01 Address an elder respectfully, not arrogantly; instead, exhort as a father, younger men as brothers.
05:02 Honor the older women as mothers, the younger ones as sisters, in all purity.
05:03 Widows honor as they should.
05:04 A widow who has children or grandchildren should first learn to manage her own household, showing hospitality to relatives and repaying debts to them; for this is acceptable before God.
05:05 The truly widowed and desolate, with hope set on God, continue in prayers and petitions night and day.
05:06 The woman living in sin has died.
05:07 Managers ought to be without reproach
05:08 If anyone does not provide for their own family, and especially for those of his household, he has denied the faith and is worse than an unbeliever.
05:09 Married woman is to be received not less than sixty years of age, one man's wife.
05:10 In good works bearing witness, if she has reared children, if she has been a hospitable host, if she has washed the feet of the holy, if she has helped those in trouble, if she has devoted herself to every good work.
05:11 Elder widows refuse. For when they have been forced into destitution because of Christ, they desire to marry.
05:12 Holding a judgment, for they have set aside the first faith.
05:13 Idle chatterers, ever learning and never able to arrive at a knowledge of the truth.
05:14 Wish therefore younger women to marry, bear children, manage a household, give no opportunity for slander to the adversary.
05:15 Already some have turned away to Satan.
05:16 If any woman has faith, let her be in quietness and control her spirit, and let not the church be burdened, for she who is truly a widow is worthy of honor.
05:17 Worthy of double honor are those who labor in the word and teaching.
05:18 The Scripture says: "You shall not muzzle the ox while it treads out the grain," and, "The laborer is worthy of his wages."
05:19 Avoid a false accusation against an elder, except in the presence of two or three witnesses.
05:20 Condemn the sinners before all, so that even others may have fear.
05:21 I testify before God and Christ Jesus, and the elect angels, that you should keep these things without prejudice, avoiding any appearance of impropriety.
05:22 Avoid hastily laying hands on anyone, nor share in other people's sins; be holy yourself.
05:23 Do not drink water any longer, but use a little wine for the sake of your stomach and frequent ailments.
05:24 Some people's sins are obvious, leading to judgment, while others continue in sin, refusing to repent.
05:25 Visibly good works cannot hide.
06:01 Those who are under the yoke, let them be subject to their own masters in all respects, so that the name of God and His teaching may not be blasphemed.
06:02 Faithful people, who have masters, do not regard them with contempt, because they are fellow believers. Instead, serve all the more, because they are worthy of respect and dearly loved, as those who may incur a debt for God's grace. Teach and encourage these things.
06:03 Contrary to one teaching unhealthy doctrines, those belonging to our Lord Jesus Christ and the holy teaching.
06:04 Blinded, knowing nothing, but sick for disputes and arguments, from which come jealousy, strife, slander, evil suspicions.
06:05 Deceitful disputes corrupting people, their minds and consciences seared from the truth, considering devotion to be mythical piety.
06:06 Great is devotion with self-sufficiency.
06:07 We brought nothing into the world, for we cannot return anything to it.
06:08 Having food and clothing, with these we will be content.
06:09 Desiring to be rich, they fall into temptation and a snare, and many foolish and harmful desires, which plunge people into ruin and destruction.
06:10 The love of money is the root of all kinds of evil, some being led astray from the faith and pierced by many sorrows.
06:11 Flee these things, you of God: pursue righteousness, godliness, faith, love, endurance, gentleness.
06:12 Fight the good fight of faith, take hold of eternal life, to which you were called and have confessed the good confession before many witnesses.
06:13 I charge you before God and Christ Jesus, who witnessed the good confession before Pontius Pilate, to keep this command without spot or blame.
06:14 Maintain the command unspotted and unreproachable until the appearing of our Lord Jesus Christ.
06:15 Who alone possesses immortality and lives in unapproachable light, whom no one has seen or can see. God, the only Wisdom, who is hidden and cannot be grasped, who rules over kings and is ruler of all. 06:17 Do not let the rich deceive themselves by thinking they will be richer still. They should instead set their hope on God, who provides us with everything for our enjoyment.
06:18 Act Righteously, Be Rich in Good Works, Be Generous, Share,
06:19 Laying up for yourselves a treasure in heaven, that you may take hold of eternal life.
06:20 Timothy, guard the deposit entrusted to you, avoiding the empty chatter and oppositions of what is falsely called knowledge.
06:21 Some have sworn concerning the faith, and perjured themselves. Grace be with you.
