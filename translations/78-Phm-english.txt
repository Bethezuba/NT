01:01 Paul, a prisoner of Christ Jesus, and Timothy our brother, To Philemon our dear friend and fellow worker,
01:02 And to Apphia, our sister, and Archippus our fellow soldier, and to the church in your house,
01:03 Grace to you and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.
01:04 I thank God for you continually, remembering you in my prayers.
01:05 Hearing your love and faith toward the Lord Jesus and all the saints.
01:06 May the fellowship of your faith become effective for everyone who is good, that you may share in every good thing in Christ.
01:07 Joy abundant I have for you, and comfort on your love, because the hearts of the saints have been refreshed through you, brother.
01:08 I have boldness in Christ to command you, [so] I command you
01:09 Through love, I appeal more urgently; just as Paul, an elderly man now also a prisoner of Christ Jesus.
01:10 I appeal to you concerning my child Onesimus, whom I have begotten in my chains.
01:11 Formerly useless to you, now indeed useful both to you and to me
01:12 My dear brother
01:13 I had hoped to keep him with me, so that he might serve you in prison for the gospel.
01:14 Apart from your will, I wished nothing to do, that not as by compulsion the good might be yours, but willingly.
01:15 Certainly for this reason I held him back, so that for eternity you may have him again.
01:16 Not any longer as a slave, but rather as a beloved brother, especially to me; yet all the more so to you both in flesh and in Lord.
01:17 Hold him as yourself
01:18 If he wronged you or owes you something, that's my concern.
01:19 I, Paul, write with my own hand, I am obligated; you also do owe me something.
01:20 Yes, brother, I entreat you in the Lord; strengthen my heart in Christ.
01:21 Trusting in your obedience, I write to you, knowing that whatever I say to you, you will do.
01:22 Immediately, I am prepared to welcome you, for I hope through your prayers I will be rewarded by you.
01:23 Greets you Epaphras, my fellow worker in Christ Jesus.
01:24 Mark, Aristarchus, Demas, Luke, my fellow workers.
01:25 Grace be to you from God our Savior Jesus Christ, according to the mercy, Blessing of the Lord Jesus Christ with you, by the mercy of your spirits. (This is a paraphrase of the verse to maintain the original structure and sense, as the direct translation might not sound natural in English.)
