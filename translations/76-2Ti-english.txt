01:01 Paul, an apostle of Christ Jesus by the will of God, according to the promise of life that is in Christ Jesus
01:02 Grace, mercy, peace from God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ our Savior.
01:03 I have a conscience clear before God and Father, from whom I do not shrink to call upon you night and day, unceasingly, regarding your remembrance in my prayers.
01:04 Longing to see you again, remembering your tears, that my joy may be made complete.
01:05 Recalled is the sincere faith that first dwelt in your grandmother Lois and your mother Eunice, and I am persuaded that it is in you as well.
01:06 I remind you to rekindle the gift of God that is in you through the imposition of my hands.
01:07 God did not give us a spirit of fear, but of power, love and self-control.
01:08 Do not be ashamed of the testimony of our Lord or of me His prisoner, but share in suffering for the Gospel according to the power of God.
01:09 Saved us and called us with a holy calling, not according to our works, but according to His own purpose and grace, which was given to us in Christ Jesus before the ages of time.
01:10 Now appearing in its fullness through the appearance of our Savior Jesus Christ, who abolished death and brought life and immortality to light through the gospel.
01:11 I was appointed a herald, an apostle, and a teacher.
01:12 Endures all these things for my sake, that I may be aided by him who strengthened me until now.
01:13 Exhibit the healthful words which you have heard from me in faith and love that is in Christ Jesus.
01:14 Maintain the pure commandment through the Holy Spirit which dwells in us.
01:15 You have understood that all in Asia turned away from me, among whom are Phygelus and Hermogenes.
01:16 The Lord be merciful to the household of Onesiphorus, for he often refreshed me and was not ashamed of my chain.
01:17 Found in Rome he sought me earnestly and found me
01:18 The Lord give him (2 Timothy 1:18) find mercy from the Lord in that day— and whatever he served in Ephesus, you know better.
02:01 Therefore, my dear child, be strong in the grace that is in Christ Jesus.
02:02 And the things you have heard from me among many witnesses, these entrust to faithful men who will be able to teach others also.
02:03 Endure suffering as a good soldier of Christ Jesus.
02:04 Nobody entangles himself in the ordinary concerns of life, so that he may please him who enlisted him as a soldier.
02:05 If anyone competes, he does not receive a crown unless he competes according to the rules.
02:06 The laboring farmer must first reap the harvest.
02:07 Grants you the Lord wisdom in all things.
02:08 Remember Jesus Christ, risen from the dead, descended from David, according to my gospel.
02:09 In which I am suffering even unto chains as a criminal, but the word of God is not bound.
02:10 Therefore I endure all things for the elect's sake, that they also may obtain the salvation which is in Christ Jesus with eternal glory.
02:11 Faithful is the word: If we die, we shall live; and if we faint, we shall not be ashamed.
02:12 If we endure, we shall reign; if we deny, he will also deny us.
02:13 If we deny, he remains faithful; he cannot deny himself.
02:14 Remember this, declaring it before the Lord, do not argue endlessly, for it is useless. Instead, aim to convert those who listen.
02:15 Strive to show yourself Approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the word of truth.
02:16 Avoid foolish and ignorant discussions. For they will lead to greater ungodliness.
02:17 Their word will be like a gnawing and corrupting rule. Of whom are Hymenaeus and Philetus.
02:18 These are those who swerved from the truth, saying that resurrection has already occurred, and they subvert the faith of some.
02:19 The firm foundation of the God remains, bearing this seal: "The Lord knows those who are His," and, "Let everyone who names the name of the Lord depart from iniquity."
02:20 In a great house there are not only vessels of gold and silver, but also of wood and clay. Some for honor, some for dishonor.
02:21 If anyone cleanses himself from these things, he will be a vessel for honor, sanctified, useful to the Master, prepared for every good work.
02:22 Flee youthful passions, pursue righteousness, faith, love, and peace with those who call on the Lord from a pure heart.
02:23 Seek to rebuke the foolish and unlearned. Knowing that they breed strife
02:24 Servant of the Lord should not strive, but be gentle towards all, teaching, patient.
02:25 Encourages the opposing ones to repentance, that perhaps God may grant them a change of heart for a recognition of truth.
02:26 And they will rise up from the devil's snares, being ensnared by him unto his will.
03:01 Difficult times will come in the last days.
03:02 Self-lovers, lovers of money, boastful, proud, blasphemers, disobedient to parents, ungrateful, unholy,
03:03 Faithless, untrustworthy, malicious, uncontrolled, lawless, selfish, unloving
03:04 Traitors, reckless, dissipated, more lovers of pleasure than lovers of God
03:05 Renouncing the form of godliness, denying its power. Turn away from such.
03:06 These are the ones who are enticing others for ungodly desires, sensuality, into homes, and are ensnaring unholy women in various sins, led by lustful passions.
03:07 Continually learning but never coming to a knowledge of the truth
03:08 These men are opposing the truth, just as Jannes and Jambres opposed Moses. Such are the corrupt in mind and rejected concerning faith.
03:09 These men will go no further; for their foolishness will be evident to all, just as was the case with those in former times.
03:10 Followed me in my teaching, the guidance, the persecutions, the faith, the longsuffering, the love, the endurance, This is the English translation of 2 Timothy 3:10 (Koine Greek), with no additional context or notes. The previous verse for context provided was 2 Timothy 3:9, which helps to understand that Paul is speaking about people who have followed him in his teachings and way of life, including persecutions he faced.
03:11 Persecutions, sufferings, such as happened to me in Antioch, Iconium, Lystra, these persecutions I endured; and the Lord delivered me from all of them.
03:12 And all who desire to live uprightly in Christ Jesus will be persecuted.
03:13 Evil men and impostors will grow worse and worse, deceiving and being deceived.
03:14 Remain in what you have learned and received, knowing from whom you learned it.
03:15 And that from childhood you have known the holy writings, which are able to make you wise for salvation through faith in Christ Jesus.
03:16 All scripture is inspired by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, for training in righteousness.
03:17 Adept is the man of God, prepared for every good work.
04:01 I am bearing witness before God and Christ Jesus, who is to judge the living and the dead, and their eternal kingdom.
04:02 Preach the word, be instant in season, out of season; reprove, rebuke, exhort with all longsuffering and doctrine.
04:03 There will be a time when they will not endure sound doctrine, but according to their own desires they will gather teachers for themselves, tuning their ears.
04:04 Turn away from the truth and turn aside to myths.
04:05 You must persevere in all things, suffer hardships, do the work of an evangelist, fulfill your ministry.
04:06 My release is at hand, and the time of my departure has arrived.
04:07 I have fought the good fight, I have finished the race, I have kept the faith
04:08 The following crown of righteousness is laid up for me, which the Lord, the righteous Judge, will award to me on that day; not only to me, but also to all who have loved His appearing.
04:09 Hurry to come to me quickly
04:10 Abandoned me is Demas, loving this present world, and went to Thessalonica; Crispus to Galatia, Titus to Dalmatia.
04:11 Luke is alone with me. Mark, receiving him, bring him with you, for he is profitable to me for service.
04:12 Sent to Ephesus was he, indeed.
04:13 Faithful is the one left in Troas, whom I left behind with Carpus, when he comes, welcome him to my room, and the books especially the parchments.
04:14 Alexander the copperworker has shown me many evil things—the Lord will repay him according to his works.
04:15 You must carefully observe that one, for he is subversive to our messages.
04:16 Abandoned me in my first defense no one did, but all forsook me.
04:17 The Lord stood by me and strengthened me, that through me the message might be fully proclaimed and heard among all the Gentiles, and I was delivered from the lion's mouth.
04:18 The Lord shall deliver me from every evil work and save me for His heavenly kingdom, to whom be glory forevermore, amen.
04:19 Greet Priscilla and Aquila, and the household of Onesiphorus.
04:20 Erastus remained in Corinth, Trophimus left sick at Miletus.
04:21 Prepare for the winter's coming. Euboulos, Pudens, Linus, Claudia, and all the brothers send greetings to you.
04:22 The Lord is with your spirit. Grace be with you.
