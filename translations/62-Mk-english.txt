14:42 Awaken, let us go; behold, the one who is betraying me has come near.
14:43 Immediately, as Jesus was still speaking, Judas, one of the twelve, arrives with a crowd carrying swords and clubs from the chief priests, scribes, and elders.
14:44 Delivered him therefore the one betraying Him to them, saying, Whom I will kiss, He is; hold Him and take Him away securely.
14:45 And coming immediately, drawing near to Him, said to Him: Rabbi, and kissed Him.
14:46 They seized Him and arrested Him.
14:47 One of those standing near drew his sword and struck the high priest's servant, cutting off his ear.
14:48 With him went out as against a robber, seizing me with swords and clubs?
14:49 I was daily with you in the temple teaching, and you did not seize me. But let the Scriptures be fulfilled.
14:50 Fled all away from him.
14:51 Young man, dressed only in a linen cloth, was following him. They seized him.
14:52 Abandons the cloak, leaving behind, naked flees
14:53 Brought Jesus to the high priest, and all the chief priests and elders and scribes were gathered.
14:54 And Peter was following from afar and entered into the courtyard of the high priest, and he was sitting with the servants and warming himself by the fire.
14:55 The high priests and the entire council were seeking testimony against Jesus to put him to death, but they found none.
14:56 Many testified falsely against him, and the testimonies were not consistent.
14:57 Rising, some falsely testified against him
14:58 We have heard him saying, "I will destroy this temple that is made without hands, and in three days I will build another not made with hands."
14:59 And their testimony was not equal
14:60 And the high priest arose to the midst and questioned Jesus, saying: Art thou able not to answer? What these witness against thee?
14:61 He remained silent and did not answer at all. Again, the high priest asked him and said to him: Art thou the Christ, the Son of the blessed?
14:62 Jesus said, "I am, and you will see the Son of Man sitting at the right hand, coming with the clouds of heaven."
14:63 The high priest tore his clothes and said, "What further need have we of witnesses?"
14:64 Heard the blasphemy; what seems this to you? But all condemned him as deserving of death.
14:65 Began some to spit on him, and to cover his face, and to strike him, and to say unto him, Prophesy; and the servants did seize him with slaps.
14:66 The woman at the temple's gate approaches Peter.
14:67 And looking at Peter as he was warming himself, she said to him: "And you also with the Nazarene were [you] the one called Jesus?"
14:68 He denied, saying, "I neither know nor acknowledge whom you are speaking about, and he went out outside to the porch. And a cock crowed."
14:69 And the maidservant, seeing him, began again to say to those who were present that This man is one of them.
14:70 He again denied. And soon after, again those present said to Peter, " Truly you are one of them, and because your speech resembles a Galilean."
14:71 He began to curse and swear, saying, "I do not know this man about whom you speak."
14:72 Immediately the rooster crowed a second time. And Peter remembered the word that Jesus had said to him, "Before the rooster crows twice, you will deny me three times." And he began to weep.
15:01 Immediately, in the morning, the leaders with the elders and scribes and the whole council bound Jesus, handed Him over to Pilate.
15:02 Pilate asked Him, "Are you the King of the Jews?" And He answered him, "You say so."
15:03 And the high priests accused Him much.
15:04 The centurion asked him again, saying, "Are you unable to speak? See how many are accusing you."
15:05 Jesus said nothing more, so the centurion marveled at him.
15:06 Released to them one prisoner, whom they were asking for.
15:07 Barabbas was with the insurrectionists, who in the insurrection had committed murder.
15:08 And the crowd began to demand as was their custom.
15:09 The Pilate answered them, saying: "Do you want to release to you the King of the Jews?"
15:10 Recognized, for envy they had delivered Him up by the chief priests.
15:11 The high priests dismissed the crowd, so they might release Barabbas to them instead.
15:12 Pilate again spoke to them, saying: "What do you want me to do with the King of the Jews?"
15:13 Crucify him.
15:14 Pilate said to them, "What evil has he done?" But they shouted all the more, "Crucify him!"
15:15 Releases Barabbas to the crowd, and delivers Jesus after scourging, so that he may be crucified.
15:16 The soldiers led him inside the palace (praetorium), and they called out the whole cohort.
15:17 And they clothe him in purple, and weave a crown of thorns, placing it on him.
15:18 Greetings, King of the Jews
15:19 And they spitted upon Him, and took the reed and struck Him; and bowing their knees worshipped Him.
15:20 And when they had mocked Him, they took the purple robe off Him and put His own clothes on Him. And they led Him away to crucify Him.
15:21 And they enlist Simon of Cyrene coming from the field, the father of Alexander and Rufus, to carry His cross.
15:22 Bring Him to the place called Golgotha, which means Skull Place.
15:23 Offered him wine mixed with myrrh, but he did not take it.
15:24 And they crucify him and divide his garments, casting lots upon them, who will take what.
15:25 Third hour, they crucified him.
15:26 The inscription of the reason for him was written: The King of the Jews.
15:27 Crucified with him were two robbers, one on his right and one on his left.
15:29 And those passing by blasphemed him, wagging their heads and saying, "Ah, the one destroying the temple and building it in three days."
15:30 Save yourself by coming down from the cross.
15:31 Similarly, the high priests mocking among themselves with the scribes said, "Let another be saved; he cannot save himself."
15:32 The King of Israel, the Christ, descends now from the cross, that we may see and believe. And those crucified with Him reviled Him.
15:33 Darkness came over the whole land for three hours.
15:34 Jesus cried out with a loud voice, "My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?"
15:35 Behold, Elijah is calling.
15:36 Running up, a certain man, having soaked sponges in wine, and wrapping a reed in them, was giving it to him to drink, saying: "Let us see if Elijah comes to save him."
15:37 Jesus breathed out a great breath.
15:38 The veil of the temple was torn in two from top to bottom.
15:39 This man was the Son of God.
15:40 Women were watching from afar, among whom were Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James the younger and of Joses, and Salome.
15:41 Following Him and serving Him in Galilee were also many others who accompanied Him into Jerusalem.
15:42 Evening came, and that day was the preparation day, which is called Sabbath
15:43 Arriving, Joseph of Arimathaea, a distinguished council member who was also himself expecting the kingdom of God, dared to enter Pilate and asked for the body of Jesus.
15:44 Pilate marveled if He already had died, and calling for the centurion, he asked him whether He had died yet.
15:45 And knowing from the centurion, he gave the corpse to Joseph.
15:46 He purchased a linen cloth, took him down, wrapped him in the linen cloth and placed him in a tomb cut out of rock, and rolled a stone against the entrance of the tomb.
15:47 The two Marys, Mary Magdalene and Mary of Joses, went to see where he was laid.
16:01 And on the Sabbath, Mary Magdalene, Mary the mother of James, and Salome bought spices that they might come and anoint him.
16:02 Very early in the morning, on the first day of the week, they came to the tomb.
16:03 Who will roll away the stone for us from the door of the tomb?
16:04 And looking up, they saw that the stone was rolled away, for it was very large.
16:05 Entered the tomb, they saw a young man sitting on the right side, dressed in a white robe; and they were amazed.
16:06 He says to them, "Do not be amazed. Seek Jesus the Nazarene who was crucified. He has risen; he is not here. See the place where they laid him."
16:07 He commands you to go and say to His disciples and to Peter that He is going before you into Galilee; there you will see Him, as He said to you.
16:09 Risen early on the first day of the week, He appeared first to Mary Magdalene, from whom He had driven out seven demons.
16:10 Announced to them, as she went away, those with him mourning and weeping
16:11 They disbelieved, having heard that she is alive and has appeared to them.
16:12 Appeared to them in a different form on their way to the country.
16:13 They reported it to the others, and they did not believe either.
16:14 Appeared later to the eleven when they were resting, and he reproached their unbelief and hardness of heart because those who saw him raised did not believe.
16:15 And he said to them: "Go into all the world and proclaim the gospel to every creature."
16:16 Believes and is baptized shall be saved; but he who does not believe shall be condemned.
16:17 Signs will accompany those who believe, in My name they will cast out demons, they will speak with new tongues.
16:18 Serpents will not harm them; on the contrary, they will lay hands on the sick and recover them.
16:19 Jesus was taken up into heaven after he had spoken to them, and sat at the right hand of God.
16:20 They went out and preached everywhere, the Lord working with them and confirming the message through the accompanying signs.
