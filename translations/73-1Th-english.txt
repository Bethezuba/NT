01:01 Paul, Silvanus, and Timothy to the church of the Thessalonians in God the Father and the Lord Jesus Christ: Grace to you and peace.
01:02 We give thanks to God always for all of you, making mention of you in our prayers, unceasingly
01:03 Remembering your work of faith, labor of love, and endurance inspired by hope in our Lord Jesus Christ, before God and Father.
01:04 Recognized, beloved brethren chosen by God,
01:05 Our gospel came to you not only in word, but also in power and in the Holy Spirit and with much conviction, just as you know, how we were among you.
01:06 You became imitators of us and of the Lord, receiving the word in much affliction, with joy of the Holy Spirit.
01:07 Your selves became an example to all the believers in Macedonia and Achaia.
01:08 From you the word of the Lord has sounded out not only in Macedonia and Achaia, but in every place your faith toward God has become manifest, so that we have no need to speak anything.
01:09 You yourselves report how we entered among you, and how you turned from idols to serve the living and true God.
01:10 And to wait for His Son from heaven, whom He raised from the dead—Jesus, who delivers us from the coming wrath.
02:01 Your translation: Yourselves, brothers, know, that our coming to you was not in vain.
02:02 Suffering and being mistreated as you know, we spoke boldly to you in God our Savior about the Gospel of God in much affliction.
02:03 Our comfort is not from deceit or impurity or cunning.
02:04 But just as we have been approved by God to speak the gospel, so we speak, not as pleasing men, but God who tests our hearts.
02:05 We never used deceitful words, nor adulterous words, as you well know. God is our witness.
02:06 Seeking glory from men, neither from you nor from others.
02:07 We were gentle among you, as a nursing mother tenderly cares for her own children.
02:08 We wish to give ourselves to you not only in the gospel of God but also in our own selves, because beloved, you have become so to us.
02:09 Remember, brethren, our labor and toil: by night and by day, working together, we brought you the gospel of God.
02:10 You are witnesses of God, and as such, we have become to you not only in word but also in deed, demonstrating ourselves to be blameless before you who believe.
02:11 As you know, we exhort each one of you as a father exhorts his own children.
02:12 Urging you, comforting you, and bearing witness to you, so that you may walk worthy of God who is calling you into His kingdom and glory.
02:13 And we also thank God continually, that receiving the word from our mouth, the word of God, which you received not as the word of men but just as it truly is the word of God, which is working in you who believe.
02:14 You have become imitators of the churches of God in Christ Jesus that are in Judea, for you also have suffered the same things from your own countrymen just as they did from the Jews.
02:15 Slaying the Lord Jesus and the prophets, and persecuting us, and displeasing God, and opposing all men.
02:16 Hindering us from speaking to the Gentiles so that they might be saved, to fill up their sins always. Nevertheless, wrath came upon them to the end.
02:17 We were bereft of you, brethren, for a short time, not in person but in deep affection; and we endeavored the more eagerly to see your face.
02:18 We wished to come to you, I (Paul) indeed, once and twice, but Satan hindered us.
02:19 Our hope, our joy, our crown of boasting— is it not you before the Lord Jesus at his coming?
02:20 You are our glory and our joy.
03:01 We prefer to remain with you (Translated from the Greek: " ")
03:02 We sent Timothy, our brother and fellow worker for God in the gospel of Christ, to establish and encourage you concerning your faith.
03:03 Nothing should be troubled by these afflictions. You yourselves know that this is our established position.
03:04 And when we were with you, we told you this: We will suffer affliction, just as it happened and you know.
03:05 I no longer persist in appeasing you, so that the testing of your faith might not be beyond what is necessary, but that it may be productive.
03:06 Upon Timothy's arrival from you, bringing news of your faith and love, and that you have a good memory of us always, longing to see us as we also long to see you.
03:07 Called upon in your midst, brothers, in every affliction and distress because of your faith.
03:08 Now if you stand fast in the Lord.
03:09 We can't express enough gratitude to God for you on account of the joy we have in your presence before our God.
03:10 Night and day, we eagerly long to see your faces and to complete what is lacking in your faith.
03:11 God (our) Father and the Lord Jesus, direct our way toward you.
03:12 The Lord overflows and abounds with love for you all, just as we do for you.
03:13 Strengthen your hearts without blame in holiness before our God and Father, at the coming of our Lord Jesus with all His holy ones.
04:01 Therefore, dear brothers and sisters, we urge you in the Lord Jesus to live in a way that pleases God, just as you are doing, so as to become more and more complete.
04:02 You know the commands we gave you through the Lord Jesus.
04:03 This is the will of God, your sanctification, that you abstain from sexual immorality.
04:04 Each of you should learn to control your own body in holiness and honor
04:05 Do not be controlled by passions of the flesh, as those who are ignorant of God.
04:06 Exceed not nor covet your brother in the matter; for the Lord is avenger of all these things, as we also previously told you, and have testified.
04:07 God called us to holiness, not to impurity.
04:08 He that disdaineth not a man, he doth disdain God, who hath given to you the Holy Spirit.
04:09 You have no need for anyone to write to you regarding brotherly love, for you yourselves have been taught by God to love one another.
04:10 You do this to all the brethren in all Macedonia. We urge you, brothers, yet more so.
04:11 Enjoy quietness, esteem it highly, and work with your own hands, as we instructed you.
04:12 Walk properly toward outsiders, and be in need of no one.
04:13 We do not want you to be unaware, brothers, concerning those who are asleep, that you may not sorrow as the rest who have no hope.
04:14 Therefore if we believe that Jesus died and rose again, even so through Jesus God will bring with him those who have fallen asleep.
04:15 This we speak to you in the Lord's words: We who are alive, remaining until the coming of the Lord, shall not precede those who have fallen asleep.
04:16 The Lord Himself will descend from heaven with the command of an archangel and the sound of God's trumpet, and the dead in Christ will rise first.
04:17 We who are alive and remain shall be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air, and so we shall always be with the Lord.
04:18 Encourage one another with these words.
05:01 Regarding times and seasons, brothers, you have no need for anything to be written to you
05:02 The Day of the Lord comes as a thief in the night.
05:03 Sudden destruction comes upon them as labor pains on a pregnant woman, and they will not escape.
05:04 You are not in darkness, brethren, that this day should overtake you as a thief.
05:05 All of you are sons of light and sons of day. We are not of night or darkness.
05:06 Therefore let us not sleep, as do others, but let us watch and be sober.
05:07 Sleeping at night, those who do; and drunkards, they drink.
05:08 We put on the breastplate of faith and love, and as a helmet, the hope of salvation.
05:09 God did not destine us for wrath but for salvation through our Lord Jesus Christ.
05:10 With Him, whether we wake or sleep, we shall live on.
05:11 Encourage one another and build each other up, just as you are doing.
05:12 We ask you, brothers, to acknowledge those who labor among you and are over you in the Lord and admonish you.
05:13 Honor them above measure through much love because of their work. Be at peace with yourselves.
05:14 Admonish the disorderly, comfort the faint-hearted, support the weak, be patient towards all.
05:15 Do not wrong one another, but rather seek the good for one another and for all.
05:16 Always rejoice
05:17 Continue earnestly praying
05:18 In everything, give thanks; for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus concerning you.
05:19 Do not quench (or extinguish) the Spirit.
05:20 Do not despise prophecies.
05:21 Test all things; hold fast to what is good.
05:22 Avoid every form of wickedness.
05:23 The God of peace sanctify you wholly, and may your whole spirit and soul and body be blameless at the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ.
05:24 Faithful is the one who calls you, who will also do it.
05:25 Brothers, pray for us
05:26 Greet all the brethren with a holy kiss.
05:27 I solemnly swear that the letter will be read to all the brethren.
05:28 The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you.
