01:01 Paul and Timothy, servants of Christ Jesus, To all the saints in Christ Jesus who are in Philippi, along with the overseers and deacons:
01:02 Grace and peace from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ.
01:03 I thank God for your memories.
01:04 Constantly in every prayer of mine for all of you, I offer the request with joy.
01:05 In partnership with you for the Gospel from the first day until now
01:06 Having confidence in this, that the one who began a good work in you will continue to complete it until the day of Christ Jesus.
01:07 This is just right for me to think this way concerning all of you, because in my heart you are all partakers of my grace. Both in my bonds and in my defense I will make it clear that all of you are participants in the gospel's grace.
01:08 Witness for me is God, as I long for all of you in the breasts of Christ Jesus.
01:09 And this I pray that your love may abound still more and more in knowledge and all discernment.
01:10 Prove yourselves authentic, that you may be approved and blameless until the day of Christ.
01:11 Filled with the fruit of righteousness, which comes through Jesus Christ, to the glory and praise of God.
01:12 I desire to know you, brethren, that what is happening concerning me has served to advance the gospel
01:13 My chains are visible in Christ, and all the more because of my imprisonment in the praetorium.
01:14 And the more numerous brethren in the Lord, having confidence in my chains, are more bold to speak the word fearlessly.
01:15 Some are preaching Christ out of envy and rivalry, but others out of goodwill.
01:16 Those from love, knowing that I am set for the defense of the gospel
01:17 Opponents are proclaiming Christ, not sincerely, thinking they cause affliction to my chains.
01:18 But indeed, Christ is being proclaimed, and I rejoice; yet I will also rejoice.
01:19 This will result in my salvation through your prayers and the operation of the Spirit of Jesus Christ.
01:20 My shame will not be apparent in any way, but in all boldness, Christ will be magnified in my body—whether by life or by death.
01:21 My living is Christ, and my dying is gain.
01:22 If living in flesh, that's the fruit of work - and what I shall choose, I don't know.
01:23 I am persuaded of this: that it is far better to depart and be with Christ; which is indeed a desirable thing.
01:24 Remain in the flesh is necessary through you.
01:25 And this I affirm, now that I know you all, remain in me and my spirit remains with you until your progress and the joy of faith.
01:26 In order for the boast of you all to abound in Christ Jesus, through my presence again among you.
01:27 Only worthy of the Gospel of Christ are you citizens, so that whether I come and see you or am absent, I hear about you that you stand firm in one spirit, with one soul striving together for the faith of the Gospel.
01:28 And not being easily ensnared in anything by opponents (which is evidence of destruction for them, but of salvation for you, and this is from God).
01:29 This has been granted to you not only to believe in Christ, but also to suffer for His sake.
01:30 Fighting the same battle as I am, you saw that in me and hear it now from me.
02:01 If anyone has compassion and sympathy in Christ Jesus, if anyone shares the Spirit and fellowship, if anyone has a heart and concerns:
02:02 Complete my joy so that you may think the same way, having the same love, being in agreement, thinking the same thing.
02:03 Consider each other superior to yourselves, not looking out for your own interests, but each of you also for the interests of the others.
02:04 Look not each out for his own interests, but also for the interests of others.
02:05 Consider these things in yourselves, which also you learned in Christ Jesus.
02:06 Emptied (In the form of God, existing, did not consider equality with God as something to be grasped)
02:07 He emptied Himself, taking the form of a servant, being born in the likeness of men. And being found in appearance as a man.
02:08 He humbled Himself, becoming obedient to death—even death on a cross.
02:09 Therefore also God highly exalted Him, and bestowed on Him the name which is above every name.
02:10 Every knee shall bow in the name of Jesus, of heaven, earth, and under the earth.
02:11 And every tongue will confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.
02:12 Work, dear ones, just as you have always obeyed, not only in my presence, but now much more in my absence, with fear and trembling, you are working for your own salvation.
02:13 God is working in you, both to will and to work for His good pleasure.
02:14 Do nothing out of selfish ambition or vain conceit. Instead, in humility consider others better than yourselves.
02:15 Blameless and innocent, children of God without blemish, in the midst of a crooked and perverse generation, among whom you shine as lights in the world.
02:16 Sharing the message of life, to my glory on the day of Christ, that I did not run in vain nor labor in vain.
02:17 But if I am poured out as a drink offering on the sacrifice and service of your faith, I rejoice and share my joy with all of you.
02:18 You yourselves rejoice, and I also rejoice with you.
02:19 I hope in the Lord Jesus to send Timothy to you soon, so that I may be encouraged, knowing nothing about you.
02:20 I have no one who truly cares about you as do I.
02:21 All seek their own, not the things of Jesus Christ.
02:22 Recognize his trial, for as a child with me he served in the gospel.
02:23 I expect to send this one, as soon as I find something that is secure concerning myself.
02:24 I have faith in the Lord that he himself will also quickly come.
02:25 Send Epaphroditus to you (Translated from Greek: , , )
02:26 Longing for all of you, and grieved because I heard that you heard he was ill.
02:27 Therefore he has been near death; but God had mercy on him, not only on him, but also on me, that there may be no further grief added to my grief.
02:28 Therefore I have sent him more eagerly, that seeing him again you may rejoice, and I may be less sorrowful.
02:29 Welcome him therefore in the Lord with all joy, and honor such persons.
02:30 Because of the work of Christ He came even to death, proclaiming to the soul in order to complete what is lacking in your service towards me.
03:01 Remaining, my brethren, rejoice in the Lord. What I am writing to you is not timid but rather certain.
03:02 Observe the dogs, observe the wicked workers, observe the destruction.
03:03 We are circumcision, we serve by the Spirit of God and boast in Christ Jesus, and not confident in the flesh.
03:04 Although I have confidence also in the flesh, yet I would rather boast in my humble circumstances.
03:05 Jewish, of the tribe of Benjamin, a Hebrew, according to the law a Pharisee.
03:06 Pursuing the church with zeal, justified by the law blameless.
03:07 Loss became for me, regarding Christ, gain.
03:08 I count all things to be loss for the sake of Christ Jesus Lord my own, on account of whom I counted all things as refuse, that I may gain Christ.
03:09 I will be found in Him, not having a righteousness of my own, but the righteousness that comes by faith in Christ, the righteousness from God based on faith.
03:10 Knowing Him and the power of His resurrection and the fellowship of His sufferings, becoming like Him in death.
03:11 If possibly I attain to the resurrection from among the dead.
03:12 Pursue what you have already obtained, that I may also be found in Him.
03:13 Brothers, I do not consider myself yet to have taken hold of it. Yet, what we have already obtained, with regard to the things behind, I forget, but reaching forward to those things ahead, I pursue as well.
03:14 Pursuing the prize of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus.
03:15 Those who are mature, this we think, and if in any other way you think, that also God will reveal to you
03:16 Join in following my example, and observe those who walk according to the pattern we gave you.
03:17 Follow my example, and imitate those who, as you have a pattern in us.
03:18 Many are walking whom I often told you about; now, even as I grieve, I say, the enemies of the cross of Christ.
03:19 Their end is destruction, whose God is their belly, and whose glory is in their shame, who mind earthly things.
03:20 Our citizenship is in heaven, from which we eagerly wait for a Savior, the Lord Jesus Christ.
03:21 Who will transform the body of our humble state into conformity with the body of His glory, by the working ability to do so, and subject all things to Himself.
04:01 Therefore, my beloved and longed-for brethren, rejoice in the Lord, cherished ones.
04:02 I beseech Euodia and Syntyche to think the same in the Lord.
04:03 I also appeal to you, helper of Clement, join with them, who worked together with me in the gospel, whose names are in the book of life.
04:04 Rejoice in the Lord always; again I will say, rejoice.
04:05 Your kindness is known to all people. The Lord is near.
04:06 Do not worry about anything, but in everything, through prayer and petition with thanksgiving, let your requests be made known to God.
04:07 The peace of God, which surpasses all understanding, will guard your hearts and minds in Christ Jesus.
04:08 Consider all that is true, noble, just, pure, lovely, commendable, if there is any excellence or praise, think about these things.
04:09 Learn and receive, hear and see these things in me, do them; and the God of peace will be with you.
04:10 Delighted greatly in the Lord that you have already thought of me, and you also think this at the right time.
04:11 Not because I lack, but I have learned to be content.
04:12 I have learned to be abased, and I have learned to abound: everywhere and in all things I am able to be content.
04:13 I am able in all things.
04:14 You have translated well, sharing in my affliction.
04:15 You yourselves, Philippians, know that at the first preaching of the gospel, when I departed from Macedonia, no church shared with me concerning a gift and a fellowship in giving and receiving, except you only.
04:16 You sent to me once and twice, to my need, to Thessalonica.
04:17 I seek not just the gift, but the increase of the fruit for your account.
04:18 I hold all things as loss for the sake of Christ. Indeed, I count everything as waste, so that I may gain Christ and be found in him, not having a righteousness derived from the law but that which comes through faith in Christ—a righteousness recognized by God, and considered worthy of God's acceptance, an offering pleasing to God. (Philippians 3:8-9, for context) English Translation: I hold all things as loss; indeed, I count everything as dung so that I may gain Christ, and be found in him, not having a righteousness derived from the law but that which comes through faith in Christ—a righteousness recognized by God, and considered worthy of God's acceptance, an offering pleasing to God.
04:19 God will supply all your needs according to the wealth of Him in glory in Christ Jesus.
04:20 To God and Father be glory forevermore. Amen.
04:21 Greet every saint in Christ Jesus. Those who are with me, the brethren, greet you.
04:22 All the saints send you greetings, especially those from Caesar's household.
04:23 The grace of the Lord Jesus Christ be with you.
