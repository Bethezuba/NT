01:01 James, a servant of God and the Lord Jesus Christ, to the twelve tribes scattered among the nations: Greetings.
01:02 All joy to you, my brothers, when you are enmeshed in various trials.
01:03 Your trial is testing the genuineness of your faith.
01:05 If anyone among you lacks wisdom, let him ask from the One giving to all generously and without rebuke, and it will be given to him.
01:06 Ask in faith, without doubting, for the one who doubts is like a wave of the sea, blown and tossed by the wind.
01:07 Does not the man suppose that he will receive something from the Lord?
01:08 Double-minded man, unstable in all his ways.
01:09 Exalt, [therefore], the brother who is humble in his high position.
01:10 The rich in his humble state will fade away, like a flower of the field.
01:11 The sun, with its scorching heat, dries up the grass; its flower falls and its beautiful appearance is changed. So it is with the wealthy man in his lifetime—he will fade away.
01:12 Blessed is the man who perseveres under trial, because when he has been tested, he will receive the crown of life that the Lord promised to those who love him.
01:13 Nobody, being tempted, should say that he is tempted by God; for God cannot be tempted with evil, and He Himself tempts no one.
01:14 Each one is tempted by his own desire, being drawn away and enticed by it.
01:15 Desire gives birth to sin, and sin gives birth to death.
01:16 Do not be deceived, my beloved brothers.
01:17 Every generous act and every perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shadow due to change.
01:18 Desired and brought forth us by truth, to be a firstfruit of his creatures.
01:19 My beloved brethren, let every person be swift to hear, slow to speak, slow to anger.
01:20 Anger does not produce the righteousness of God.
01:21 Laying aside all bitterness, anger, wrath, and malice, welcome one another as you would welcome the Messenger of righteousness. This message is powerful enough to save your souls.
01:22 Become doers of the word, and not hearers only deceiving yourselves.

01:24 Forgot himself and immediately fell away, being the man he was.
01:25 This man, who has looked into the perfect law of freedom and continues, not becoming a hearer of the word but a doer of deeds, this one will be blessed in his work.
01:26 Deceitful is the religion of one who claims to be religious but deceives his own heart.
01:27 Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this: to visit orphans and widows in their trouble, and to keep oneself unspotted from the world.
02:01 Have no arrogance, brothers, regarding the faith of our Lord Jesus Christ.
02:02 If a man wearing golden rings and fine clothing comes into your assembly, yet a poor man in shabby clothes also comes in,
02:03 Look at the one wearing the fine clothing and say, "Sit here in a good place," and to the poor man say, "Stand there [or sit] by my footstool."
02:04 You did not discriminate among yourselves and became judges of wicked discussions.
02:05 Hear, my beloved brethren. Has not God chosen the poor in the world to be rich in faith and heirs of the kingdom which He promised to those who love Him?
02:06 You have shown favor to the poor. Do not the rich oppress you, and drag you into the courts of law?
02:07 They do not blaspheme the good name that is called upon you.
02:08 If you fulfill the royal law according to the scripture, "You shall love your neighbor as yourself," you do well.
02:09 If you show partiality, you commit sin, being condemned by the law as transgressors.
02:10 Whoever keeps the whole law and yet stumbles in one point, has become accountable for all of it.
02:11 He who says, 'Do not commit adultery,' also says, 'Do not murder.' If you do not commit adultery but murder, you have become a lawbreaker.
02:12 So also you speak and so you do as if for the sake of freedom through the law; however, you are enslaved by it.
02:13 The judgment is unmerciful to the one who does not show mercy; mercy triumphs over judgment.
02:14 Faith without works is useless, my brothers. Can faith save one who has no works?
02:15 If a brother or sister lack clothing and daily food,
02:16 Provide them with what is necessary for the body, but why ask?
02:17 Faith, if it does not have works, is dead to itself.
02:18 You will say to me, "You have faith and I have works." Show me your faith apart from your works, and I will show you my faith by my works.
02:19 You believe that there is one God; you do well. Even the demons believe and shudder.
02:20 Desires thou to know, O man, neither art thou, that faith without works is dead?
02:21 Abraham our father was not declared righteous by works, offering up Isaac his son on the altar?
02:22 Faith works together with works, and by works faith is completed.
02:23 And was fulfilled the scripture which says: "Abraham believed God, and he was accounted to him for righteousness, and he was called the friend of God."
02:24 A man is justified by works, and not by faith alone.
02:25 The same is not the case with Rahab the prostitute, was she not justified by works when she welcomed the messengers and sent them out another way?
02:26 The same as a body without a spirit is dead, so faith without deeds is dead.
03:01 Not many teachers, my brethren, become such, knowing that a greater judgment we shall receive.
03:02 We all stumble in many ways. If anyone does not stumble in his speech, he is a perfect man, able also to bridle the whole body.
03:03 If we control the horses' mouths to make them obey us, we can steer the entire body as well.
03:04 And behold, even ships, though they be so great and carried by harsh winds, are turned by the smallest rudder at the will of the steersman.
03:05 Thus also the tongue is a small member, yet it boasts greatly.
03:06 And the tongue is a fire, the world of unrighteousness; the tongue is set among our members, stirring up the whole body, and setting on fire the course of nature, and being set on fire by hell.
03:07 All flesh is subject to mankind's dominion (The Greek original: "Pasa gar physis therion te kai peteinon herpeton te kai enalion damazetai kai dedamastai tē physei tē anthropini.")
03:08 Man cannot tame the tongue; it is unruly and a source of deceit.
03:09 Bless in her the Lord and Father, and curse in her the people who are according to God's likeness become.
03:10 From the same mouth comes blessing and cursing. My brothers, these things should not be so.
03:11 Why does a spring produce both sweet and bitter water?
03:12 Cannot, my brothers, make a fig tree bear olives, or a vine produce figs. Neither can salt water become sweet.
03:13 Who among you is wise and understanding? Let him show it by his good life, in deeds done in mercy.
03:14 Harbor bitter jealousy and selfish ambition in your hearts, do not boast about it or lie against the truth.
03:15 This is not wisdom from above, but earthly, sensual, demonic.
03:16 Where jealousy and strife exist, there is disorder and every evil work.
03:17 The wisdom from above is first pure, then peaceable, gentle, willing to yield, full of mercy and good fruits, impartial and sincere.
03:18 Sown in peace by the seeds of righteousness.
04:01 From where do wars and fights among you come? Do they not come from your desires for pleasure that are at war within you?
04:02 You desire, yet you have not; you kill and covet, yet you cannot obtain; you fight and war. You do not have because you do not ask.
04:03 Ask and do not receive, because you ask amiss, that you may spend it on your pleasures.
04:04 Adulteresses, do you not know that friendship with the world is enmity with God? Whoever therefore wants to be a friend of the world makes himself an enemy of God.
04:05 Does it say in the Scripture, "The Spirit that He caused to dwell in us is envied?"
04:06 God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble.
04:07 Submit to God, resist the devil, and he will flee from you.
04:08 Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you. Cleanse your hands, sinners, and purify your hearts, double-minded.
04:09 Mourn and weep and wail. Let your laughter be turned to mourning and your joy to gloom.
04:10 Humble yourselves before the Lord, and he will lift you up.
04:11 Do not speak evil against one another, brethren. He who speaks evil against a brother or judges his brother, speaks evil about the law and judges the law. But if you judge the law, you are not a doer of the law but a judge.
04:12 One is a lawgiver and a judge, one who can save and destroy. But who are you to judge another?
04:13 Go now, you who say: Today or tomorrow we will go to this city and spend a year there, doing business and making a profit.
04:14 You do not know what tomorrow will bring. You are merely a vapor that appears for a little while and then disappears.
04:15 Instead of saying to you: If the Lord wills, we shall live and do this or that.
04:16 Now boast in your arrogance; every such boasting is evil.
04:17 Good is to do and not to do is sinning to him.
05:01 Weep now, the rich, weep and wail for your impending troubles.
05:02 Your wealth has rotted, and your fine garments have become moth-eaten.
05:03 Your gold and silver are corroded, And their rust will be a testimony to you, And will eat your flesh like fire; You have stored up treasure in the last days.
05:04 The reward of the workers who have not cultivated your lands is crying out, and the cries of the harvesters have reached the ears of the Lord of Sabath.
05:05 You have been carried away with the world and have lived luxuriously, you have cultivated your hearts in a day of slaughter.
05:06 You have judged the just, you have killed the righteous. Does it not oppose you?
05:07 Endure therefore, brethren, until the coming of the Lord. Behold, the farmer waits for the precious produce of the soil, being patient with it until he receives the early and the late rain.
05:08 Endure therefore, and strengthen your hearts, for the coming of the Lord is at hand.
05:09 Do not grumble, brethren, against one another, that ye may not be judged: behold, the Judge standeth before the doors.
05:10 Take as an example, my brothers, those of prophets who spoke in the name of the Lord.
05:11 Blessed are those who endure, for they will find favor with the Lord, because He is full of compassion and merciful.
05:12 Before all things, my brothers, do not swear—neither by heaven nor by earth nor by any other oath. But let your 'Yes' be 'Yes,' and your 'No,' 'No,' so that you may not fall under judgment.
05:13 Suffers one among you? Let him pray. Rejoices one among you? Let him sing.
05:14 Let him call for the elders of the church, and let them pray over him, anointing him with oil in the name of the Lord.
05:15 And the prayer of faith shall save the sick, and the Lord shall raise him up; if he has committed sins, he shall be forgiven him.
05:16 Confess your sins to one another, and pray for each other, so that you may be healed. The prayer of a righteous person is powerful in its effect.
05:17 Elijah was a man with a nature like ours, and he prayed earnestly that it might not rain; and it did not rain on the earth for three years and six months.
05:18 Again he prayed, and the heaven gave rain, and the earth produced its fruit.
05:19 Return my brethren, if anyone strays from truth and turns back,
05:20 Saves a soul from death and covers multitudes of sins.
