01:01 In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.
01:02 This was in the beginning with God.
01:03 Everything was made through Him, and without Him nothing was made that was made.
01:04 In Him was life, and the life was the light of men.
01:05 And the light shines in darkness, and the darkness did not comprehend it.
01:06 A man was sent from God, named John.
01:07 This came for testimony, that he might testify concerning the light, that all might believe through him.
01:08 He was not the light, but to testify concerning the light.
01:09 The true light, which enlightens everyone coming into the world, was coming.
01:10 In the world was, and the world was through Him created, and the world did not know Him.
01:11 Came to His own, and His own did not receive Him.
01:12 Those who received Him were given the right to become children of God, to those who believe in His name.
01:13 Born not of blood, nor of the will of the flesh, nor of the will of man, but of God.
01:14 And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us, and we have seen his glory, glory as of the only Son from the Father, full of grace and truth.
01:15 This bears witness concerning him and cried out, saying, "He who comes after me ranks before me, for he was before me."
01:16 We have received grace upon grace.
01:17 The law was given through Moses, but grace and truth came through Jesus Christ.
01:18 No one has ever seen God, but the One who is at the Father's side—He has made Him known.
01:19 And this is the testimony of John when the Jews sent priests and Levites from Jerusalem to ask him: Who are you?
01:20 He confessed and did not deny, and he confessed that "I am not the Christ."
01:21 Are you Elijah? And he said, No. The prophet are you? And he replied, No.
01:22 Said to him: Who are you? That we may give an answer to those who sent us: What do you say about yourself?
01:23 Announced: I am a voice crying out in the wilderness: Prepare the way of the Lord, as Isaiah the prophet said.
01:24 And they were sent from the Pharisees.
01:25 They asked him, "What are you baptizing, if you are not the Christ, nor Elijah, the prophet?"
01:26 He replied to them John, saying: I baptize in water; among you stands one whom you do not know.
01:27 The one coming after me is mightier; I am not worthy to untie the thong of his sandal.
01:28 This occurred in Bethany beyond the Jordan, where John was baptizing.
01:29 The next day he sees Jesus coming to him, and says, "Behold, the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world.
01:30 This is he of whom I said, "After me comes a man who has a higher rank than I, for he was before me."
01:31 Came I, baptizing with water. (John 1:31, second half of the verse)
01:32 And John testified saying, "I have seen the Spirit descending as a dove from heaven, and it remained upon him."
01:33 That one whom you see the Spirit descending and remaining upon, he it is that baptizes with the Holy Spirit.
01:34 This one is the chosen of God.
01:35 The next day again John stood
01:36 Behold the Lamb of God.
01:37 Following Jesus were the two disciples who heard Him speaking.
01:38 Turning then, Jesus saw them following and said to them, "What are you seeking?" And they said to Him, "Rabbi" (which is translated Teacher), "where are You staying?"
01:39 They went and found where He was staying, and stayed with Him that day. It was about the tenth hour.
01:40 Andrew, the brother of Simon Peter, was one of the two who heard what John said and followed him.
01:41 This man finds his own brother Simon, and says to him, "We have found the Messiah" (which is translated Christ).
01:42 Brought him to Jesus. Looking at him, Jesus said, "You are Simon son of John; you shall be called Cephas" (which is translated Peter).
01:43 The next day Jesus decided to go to Galilee, and he found Philip and said to him, "Follow me."
01:44 Philip was from Bethsaida, a city of Andrew and Peter.
01:45 Finds Philip Nathaniel and says to him, "The one written by Moses in the law, and the prophets, we have found, Jesus, the son of Joseph, from Nazareth."
01:46 And Nathaniel said to him, "Can any good thing come out of Nazareth?" Philip says to him, "Come and see."
01:47 Jesus saw Nathanael coming to him, and said about him, "Truly, an Israelite in whom there is no deceit."
01:48 Asks Nathanael: How do you know me? Jesus replied and said to him: Before Philip called you, under the fig tree I saw you.
01:49 Nathaniel answered Him: "Rabbi, you are the Son of God; you are the King of Israel."
01:50 Jesus answered and said to him, "Because you said to me, 'I saw you under the fig tree,' do you believe?" He replied, "Behold!"
01:51 And he says to him, "Truly, truly, I say to you, you will see heaven opened, and the angels of God ascending and descending upon the Son of Man."
02:01 And on the third day there was a wedding in Cana of Galilee, and the mother of Jesus was there.
02:02 Called also Jesus and his disciples to the wedding.
02:03 Running out of wine, his mother tells him, "They have no more wine."
02:04 Jesus said to her, "Woman, what is that to me and you? My hour has not yet come."
02:05 The mother of Jesus said to the disciples: What are you about to do?
02:06 Large stone jars, used for Jewish purification, were there, each containing two or three measures.
02:07 Fill the jars with water. And they filled them up to the brim.
02:08 Bring now and carry to the chief steward. They brought.
02:09 The chief steward tasted the water that had become wine, and he did not know where it was from, but the servants who had drawn the water knew, so the chief steward called the bridegroom.
02:10 And he says to him: Every man sets aside the good wine first, and when men have become drunk with the lesser, you have kept the good wine until now.
02:11 Jesus began His signs at Cana of Galilee and revealed His glory, and His disciples believed in Him.
02:12 After this, He went down to Capernaum Himself, and His mother, and His brothers, and His disciples, and they stayed there for a few days.
02:13 Jesus approached the Passover of the Jews, and He went up to Jerusalem.
02:14 Found in the temple those selling oxen, sheep, and doves, and the money-changers sitting.
02:15 And he drove out all those who were selling and buying in the temple, and overturned the tables of the money-changers and the seats of those who sold doves.
02:16 And he said to those who sold pigeons, "Take these things away; do not make my Father's house a marketplace."
02:17 The disciples remembered that it is written: "The zeal of thy house hath eaten me."
02:18 They answered and said to Him, "What sign showest thou unto us, seeing that thou doest these things?"
02:19 Jesus answered and said to them, "Destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up."
02:20 They said to Him, "Fforty-six years ago was this temple built, and You will rebuild it in three days?"
02:21 That one spoke about the temple of His body.
02:22 When he therefore was raised from the dead, his disciples remembered that he had said this, and believed in the scripture and in the word which Jesus had spoken.
02:23 In the Passover and the Feast of Unleavened Bread in Jerusalem, many believed in his name after seeing the signs he performed.
02:24 He himself did not trust them, because he knew all people.
02:25 And he needed not that any should testify concerning man, for he himself knew what was in man.
03:01 The man was from the Pharisees, named Nicodemus, a ruler of the Jews.
03:02 This man came to Him at night and said to Him, "Rabbi," (he said), "we know that You have come from God as a teacher; for no one can perform these signs that You do unless God is with him."
03:03 Jesus answered and said to him, "Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born anew, he cannot see the kingdom of God."
03:04 Cannot a man be born when he is old? Can he enter again into his mother's womb and be born?
03:05 Jesus answered, "Truly, truly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God."
03:06 The flesh is born of the flesh, and the spirit is born of the Spirit.
03:07 You shall be born again.
03:08 The Spirit breathes where it will, and you hear its voice, but you do not know from where it comes or where it goes; so is everyone who is born of the Spirit.
03:09 Nikodemus replied and said to Him, "How can these things be?"
03:10 You are a teacher of Israel, and yet you do not know these things?
03:11 We bear witness to what we know, and the testimony which we give is not received by you.
03:12 If you do not believe what I said about earthly things, how can you believe if I tell you about heavenly things?
03:13 And no one has ascended into heaven except the one who descended from heaven, the Son of Man.
03:14 And just as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, so must the Son of Man be lifted up.
03:15 Has faith in him shall have eternal life.
03:16 Therefore the love of God was toward the world, that He gave His only begotten Son, so that everyone who believes in Him should not perish, but have eternal life.
03:17 Did not send the God the Son into the world to judge the world, but that the world might be saved through Him.
03:18 The one who believes in Him is not judged; the one who does not believe has already been judged, because he did not believe in the name of the only begotten Son of God.
03:19 This is the judgment: that the light has come into the world, and people loved darkness rather than light, for their deeds were evil.
03:20 Everyone who does what is evil hates the light and avoids it, so that their deeds may not be exposed.
03:21 The one doing the truth comes to the light, so that his works may be clearly seen as having been done in God.
03:22 After this, Jesus went with his disciples to the land of Judea, and there he stayed with them and baptized.
03:23 John was also baptizing at Aenon near Salim, because there was much water, and people were coming to him and being baptized.
03:24 Not yet arrested was John.
03:25 The request came from John's disciples to a Jew regarding purification.
03:26 And there went to John, and said unto him, Rabbi, he that was with thee beyond Jordan, to whom thou bearest witness, behold, this one baptizeth, and cometh all to him.
03:27 John answered and said, "A man cannot receive anything unless it is given him from heaven."
03:28 You testify about me that I said, "I am not the Christ, but I have been sent ahead of him."
03:29 The groom's friend, rejoicing, rejoices also at the bridegroom's voice; now this my joy is complete.
03:30 He must increase, I must decrease.
03:31 The one coming from above is greater than all. He who is from the earth belongs to the earth and speaks of the earth. He who comes from heaven is over all.
03:32 He who has seen and heard has borne witness, and his testimony no one accepts.
03:33 He who has received his testimony has certified that God is truthful.
03:34 The one whom God sent speaks the words of God; for He does not give the Spirit by measure.
03:35 The Father loves the Son, and has given all things into His hand.
03:36 Believes in the Son has eternal life; but he who does not believe in the Son will not see life, but the wrath of God remains on him.
04:01 Therefore, Jesus knew that the Pharisees had heard that He was baptizing more disciples than John.
04:02 Jesus himself was not baptizing, but his disciples.
04:03 Went again into Galilee.
04:04 Must Jesus pass through Samaria.
04:05 Arrives then in the city of Samaria, called Sychar, near the field which Jacob gave to Joseph his son.
04:06 Jacob's well was there. So Jesus, wearied from His journey, sat thus at the well; it was about the sixth hour.
04:07 Woman from Samaria comes to draw water. Jesus says to her, "Give me drink."
04:08 The disciples of Jesus had left for the city to buy food.
04:09 The Samaritan woman says to Him, "How is it that you, being a Jew, ask me for a drink seeing I am a woman of Samaria? For Jews do not associate with Samaritans."
04:10 Jesus answered her, "If you knew the gift of God and who it is that is saying to you, 'Give me a drink,' you would have asked him, and he would have given you living water."
04:11 She says to him, "Lord, you have no bucket, and the well is deep; where then do you have living water?"
04:12 Are you greater than our father Jacob, who gave us the well and drank from it himself, along with his sons and his livestock?
04:13 Jesus answered her, "Whoever drinks from this water will thirst again."
04:14 Whoever drinks from the water that I will give him shall never thirst, but the water that I will give him will become in him a well of water springing up to eternal life.
04:15 The woman says to Him, "Give me this water, so that I may never thirst, nor have to come here to draw water."
04:16 Go, call your husband, and come here.
04:17 The woman answered him and said, "I have no husband." Jesus said to her, "You have spoken well: 'I have no husband.'"
04:18 Five husbands you have had, and the one whom you now have is not your husband. This you have spoken truly.
04:19 You woman say to him: Lord, I perceive that you are a prophet.
04:20 Our fathers worshiped in this mountain, and you say that in Jerusalem is the place where it is necessary to worship.
04:21 Jesus says to her, "Truly, truly, a time is coming when neither on this mountain nor in Jerusalem will you worship the Father."
04:22 You worship what you do not know; we worship what we do know, for salvation is from the Jews.
04:23 The time is coming, and it is now, when the genuine worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth, for such are the ones the Father seeks to worship Him.
04:24 The Spirit is the one from God, and those who worship Him must do so in spirit and truth.
04:25 He tells him the woman, I know that Messiah is coming, who is called Christ; when he comes, he will tell us all.
04:26 Jesus says to her, "I am, the one who is speaking to you."
04:27 And at this, his disciples came, and they marveled that he was speaking with a woman. None of them however said: What seekest thou? Or what speakest thou with her?
04:28 Left her waterpot, the woman went into the city and tells the people.
04:29 See a man who said to me all that I did. Is this the Christ?
04:30 Left the city and went towards Him.
04:31 Eat, Rabbi
04:32 I said to them, "I have food to eat that you do not know about."
04:33 Bringed him something to eat, they said to one another.
04:34 Jesus says to them, "My food is to do the will of Him who sent me and to complete His work."
04:35 Are you not saying that there is still four months until the harvest? I tell you, lift up your eyes and look at the fields, for they are white for harvest. Already.
04:36 The one reaping a wage also is gathering fruit for eternal life, so that the sower and the reaper may rejoice together.
04:37 In this, the word is true: Another one sows and another reaps.
04:38 I sent you to reap what you have not labored for; others labored and you have entered into their labor.
04:39 Many from that city believed in Him because of the woman's testimony, saying, "He told me all that I have done."
04:40 Stayed there two days
04:41 And many more believed through his word
04:42 She no longer believes through your words, but we have heard for ourselves, and we know that this is indeed the Savior of the world.
04:43 Left thereafter for two days, he went into Galilee
04:44 Jesus declared that a prophet has no honor in his own hometown.
04:45 Upon coming to Galilee, the Galileans received him, seeing all that he had done in Jerusalem during the feast; and they themselves went up to the feast.
04:46 Returned again to Cana of Galilee, where he made water into wine. And there was a royal official whose son was sick at Capernaum.
04:47 This man, having heard that Jesus had come from Judea to Galilee, went to Him and asked Him to come down and heal his son, for he was about to die.
04:48 Jesus said to him, "Unless you see signs and wonders, you will not believe."
04:49 The king says to him, "Lord, let the child live before he dies."
04:50 Jesus says to him, "Your son lives." The man believed the word that Jesus spoke and went on his way.
04:51 His servant(s) met him on his way, saying that his son lives.
04:52 He learned then the hour in which he would be raised, and they said to him that on the sixth day his fever left him.
04:53 Then the Father knew that in that hour which Jesus had spoken to him, "Your son lives," and he believed, and his whole house.
04:54 This is again the second sign that Jesus performed coming from Judea to Galilee.
05:01 After this was the feast of the Jews, and Jesus went up to Jerusalem.
05:02 There is in Jerusalem near the Sheep Gate a pool, which is called in Hebrew Bethzatha, having five porticoes.
05:03 A multitude of invalids - blind, lame, and paralyzed - lay there.
05:05 There was a man there who had been ill for thirty-eight years.
05:06 Jesus seeing him lying there, and knowing that he had been ill for a long time, says to him, "Do you want to be made well?"
05:07 The man answered him, "Sir, I have no one to put me into the pool when the water is stirred up. While I am coming, another steps down before me."
05:08 Jesus says to him, "Rise, take up your bed and walk."
05:09 Immediately, the man was made well, took up his bed, and walked. It was on that day a Sabbath.
05:10 The Jews said to the healed man: "It's a Sabbath; it is not lawful for you to carry your bed."
05:11 That one replied to them, "He who made me well told me, 'Pick up your bed and walk.'"
05:12 Who is the man who said to you, "Pick up your bed and walk"?
05:13 The healed man did not know who he was, for Jesus had departed among the crowd that was in the place.
05:14 Jesus finds him in the temple and said to him, "See, you are made well; sin no more, so that something worse does not happen to you."
05:15 The man departed and told the Jews that Jesus is the one making him well.
05:16 The Jews persecuted Jesus because He was doing these things on the Sabbath.
05:17 My Father continues to work, and I also am working.
05:18 Therefore more the Jews sought to kill him, because not only he broke the Sabbath, but also called his Father, making himself equal with God.
05:19 Jesus answered them, "Truly, truly, I say to you, the Son can do nothing of himself, unless he sees the Father doing something. Whatever the Father does, that the Son also does in like manner."
05:20 The Father loves the Son and shows him all that he himself does; and greater works than these will He show him, that you may marvel.
05:21 The Father raises the dead and makes them alive; so also does the Son give life to whom He wills.
05:22 The Father does not judge anyone, but He has given all judgment to the Son.
05:23 All should honor the Son as they honor the Father. He who does not honor the Son does not honor the Father who sent Him.
05:24 Whoever believes in my message has eternal life and will not come into judgment but has already passed from death to life.
05:25 The dead will hear the voice of the Son of God, and those who hear will live.
05:26 Also the Son has life in himself.
05:27 And he gave him authority to judge, because the Son of Man is.
05:28 Do not marvel at this, for the hour is coming when all who are in the tombs will hear His voice.
05:29 And those who do good will come to the resurrection of life, those who do evil to the resurrection of judgment.
05:30 I can do nothing on my own; as I hear, I judge, and my judgment is just, because I seek not my own will but the will of the one who sent me.
05:31 My testimony is not true.
05:32 Another testifies concerning me, and I know that his testimony is true.
05:33 Sent to John, and he bore witness to the truth.
05:34 I do not receive testimony from man, but these things I say so that you may be saved.
05:35 That one was the lamp that was burning and shining, yet you chose to rejoice at the hour in his light.
05:36 I have a testimony greater than that of John. For the works which he gave me to complete, these works that I do, bear witness concerning me that the Father has sent me.
05:37 The Father who sent me has testified concerning me. Neither have you heard his voice at any time, nor seen his form.
05:38 You do not have his word abiding in you, for the one whom he sent, you do not believe.
05:39 You search the Scriptures, because you think in them you have eternal life; and these are the ones testifying concerning me.
05:40 And yet you do not want to come to me, that you may have life.
05:41 I do not seek glory from people.
05:42 You do not have the love of God in yourselves.
05:43 I have come in My Father's name, and you do not receive Me. If another comes in his own name, him you will receive.
05:44 How can you believe, receiving glory from one another, and seeking not the glory that comes from the only God?
05:45 Do not think that I will accuse you to the Father: it is Moses who accuseth you, unto whom ye have set your hope.
05:46 Moses wrote about me
05:47 If you do not believe in his writings, how will you believe in my words?

06:02 Followed Him therefore a great crowd, because they beheld the signs which He was doing among the infirm.
06:03 Jesus went to the mountain, and there He sat with His disciples.
06:04 Near was the Passover, the feast of the Jews.
06:05 Jesus raised his eyes and, seeing that a large crowd was coming to him, said to Philip, "Where shall we buy bread for these people to eat?"
06:06 This said he, testing him; for he himself knew what he was about to do.
06:07 Philip answered him, "Forty denarii are not enough for them, that each may take a little."
06:08 Andrew, Simon Peter's brother, speaks to him.
06:09 This child here has five loaves of barley bread and two fish, but what is that for so many?
06:10 Jesus said, "Make the people sit down." There was much grass in the place. So the men sat down in companies, about five thousand.
06:11 Jesus took the loaves; and when he had given thanks, he distributed to those who were seated—similarly from the fish as much as they wanted.
06:12 Gather the fragments left over, that nothing may be lost.
06:13 Collected therefore, and filled twelve baskets with fragments of the five loaves of barley grain which remained to those who had eaten.
06:14 This man is truly the prophet who is coming into the world.
06:15 Jesus therefore, knowing that they were coming to seize Him and make Him a king, withdrew again by Himself to the mountain alone.
06:16 Came down the disciples at evening to the sea
06:17 And entering into the boat, they went over the sea to Capernaum. Now darkness had come and yet Jesus had not come to them.
06:18 The sea was troubled by a great wind.
06:19 Seeing Jesus walking on the sea and approaching the boat, they became afraid.
06:20 I am here; do not be afraid.
06:21 Wished then to take him into the boat, and immediately the boat was on land where they were going.
06:22 The next day, the crowd that had stayed on the other side of the sea saw that there was no boat there, except one, and that Jesus had not boarded it, but only His disciples had gone away.
06:23 Came a boat from Tiberias near the place where they ate the bread of the Lord's thanksgiving.
06:24 When the crowd realized that Jesus was not there, nor his disciples, they entered the boats and went to Capernaum seeking Jesus.
06:25 Found Him on the other side of the sea, they said to Him, "Rabbi, when have you been here?"
06:26 You replied to them Jesus, and said: "Truly, truly, I say to you, seek me not because you saw signs, but because you ate of the loaves and were filled."
06:27 Work not for the food which perishes, but for the food which endures for eternal life, which the Son of Man will give you; for whom the Father has sealed and the God has consecrated.
06:28 What are we to do that we may work the works of God?
06:29 Believe in the one he sent.
06:30 Said to Him therefore, "What are you doing a sign, that we may see and believe You? What work are You doing?"
06:31 The men ate the manna in the wilderness, as it is written: He gave them bread from heaven to eat.
06:32 Jesus said to them, "Truly, truly, I say to you, it was not Moses who gave you the bread from heaven, but my Father gives you the true bread from heaven.
06:33 The bread that comes down from heaven is giving life to the world.
06:34 Give therefore to us this bread.
06:35 Jesus said to them, "I am the bread of life. Whoever comes to me will never hunger, and whoever believes in me will never thirst."
06:36 You have seen me, and yet you do not believe.
06:37 All that my Father gives me will come to me, and whoever comes to me I will not cast out.
06:38 Came down from heaven not to do my will but the will of him who sent me
06:39 This is the will of the one who sent me, that I should not lose anything of what he has given me, but raise it on the last day.
06:40 This is the will of my Father that whoever sees the Son and believes in him should have eternal life, and I will raise him up at the last day.
06:41 I am the bread that came down from heaven
06:42 And they said, 'Is not this Jesus, the son of Joseph, whose father and mother we know? How can he now say that he has come down from heaven?'
06:43 Jesus answered them, "Do not mourn among yourselves."
06:44 No one can come to Me unless the Father who sent Me draws him; and I will raise him up on the last day.
06:45 And is written in the prophets: And all shall be taught by God; and he that hears from the Father and learns, comes to Me.
06:46 Has not anyone seen the Father except the one who is from God? This one has seen the Father.
06:47 Truly, truly, I say to you, he who believes has eternal life.
06:48 I am the bread of life
06:49 Their fathers ate manna in the wilderness and died
06:50 This is the bread that comes down from heaven, so that one may eat of it and not die.
06:51 I am the living bread that came down from heaven. Whoever eats from this bread will live forever, and the bread that I will give is my flesh for the life of the world.

06:54 He who eats my flesh and drinks my blood has eternal life, and I will raise him up on the last day.
06:55 My flesh is true food, and my blood is true drink.
06:56 Abides in me the one who eats my flesh and drinks my blood.
06:57 Lives through me the one who eats me, and that one will also live because of me.
06:58 This is the bread that came down from heaven, not as the fathers ate and died; he who eats this bread will live forever.

06:60 Many from among his disciples said, "This message is hard." Who can listen to it?
06:61 This knowing Jesus within himself that his disciples murmur concerning this, said to them: Doth this offend you?
06:62 If you see the Son of Man ascending where he was before?
06:63 The Spirit is the one who gives life; the flesh is of no avail. The words that I have spoken to you are spirit and are life.
06:64 Some of you do not believe. Jesus knew from the beginning who were not believing, and who it was that would betray him.
06:65 And he said, "Therefore I have said to you that no one can come to me unless it has been given him from the Father."
06:66 Many from among his disciples went away to the rear, and they no longer walked with him.
06:67 Said then Jesus to the twelve: "Wilt ye also go away?"
06:68 Simon Peter replied to Him, "Lord, to whom shall we go? You have the words of eternal life."
06:69 And we have believed, and we have known that thou art the Holy One of God.
06:70 Jesus answered them, "Did I not choose you, the twelve? And one of you is a devil."
06:71 Said therefore to Judas Iscariot, for he was about to betray him, one of the twelve.

07:02 The feast of the Jews, the Passover, was near.
07:03 Go then to the Jews, and go to Judea, that your disciples also may see the works that you are doing.
07:04 Nobody does anything secretly that he might also be openly known; if you do these things, reveal yourself to the world.
07:05 Brothers do not believe in him.
07:06 Jesus said to them, "My time has not yet come, but yours is always ready."
07:07 The world cannot hate you, but it hates me because I testify about it that its works are evil.
07:08 You go up to the feast; I go not up to this feast, because my hour has not yet come.
07:09 Remained in Galilee after saying this.
07:10 As his brothers went up to the feast, then he also went up, not publicly but in secret.
07:11 They sought him at the feast and said, "Where is he?"
07:12 Great discontent about him was among the crowds. Some said, "He is good," but others said, "No, he leads astray."
07:13 Nobody spoke openly about him due to the fear of the Jews.
07:14 Jesus ascended to the temple during the feast and taught.
07:15 They wondered, saying, "How does this man know scriptures without having been educated?"
07:16 Jesus answered them, "My teaching is not mine but his who sent me."
07:17 If anyone wants to do God's will, he will know concerning my teaching whether it is from God or I speak on my own accord.
07:18 Seeks his own glory speaks; but he seeking the glory of the one who sent him, that one is true, and in him is no unrighteousness.

21:04 Dawn had already come, and Jesus was standing on the shore; but the disciples did not know that it was Jesus.
21:05 Jesus said to them, "Children, do you have any fish?" They answered him, "No."
21:06 Cast the net on the right side of the boat, and you will find some. So they cast it, and now they were not able to draw it in because of the multitude of fish.
21:07 The disciple whom Jesus loved says, "The Lord is it." Therefore Simon Peter, hearing that the Lord is it, put on his outer garment for he was naked and threw himself into the sea.
21:08 The other disciples came to the boat, for they were not far from land – about two hundred cubits away. They were drawing in the fish with their net.
21:09 They see a coal lying there and fish and bread.
21:10 Bring the fish you have caught.
21:11 Rises then Simon Peter and draws the net into the land full of great fishes, one hundred and fifty-three; and without being broken was the net.
21:12 Jesus says to them, "Come and breakfast." None of the disciples dared ask him, "Who are you?" Knowing that it is the Lord.
21:13 Jesus comes and takes the bread and gives it to them, and the fish likewise.
21:14 Third time Jesus appeared to his disciples, risen from the dead.
21:15 Jesus says to Simon Peter, "Simon son of John, do you love me more than these?" He said to him, "Yes, Lord, you know that I love you." Jesus said to him, "Feed my sheep."

21:19 This said he, signifying by what manner of death he should glorify God. And when he had said this, he said to him, Follow me.
21:20 Returning, Peter saw the disciple whom Jesus loved following, who also leaned back on His breast at the supper and said, "Lord, who is it that is betraying You?"
21:21 This man, Lord, is it?
21:22 Jesus says to him, "If he wishes to remain until I come, what is that to you? You follow me."
21:23 The Word left for the brothers that the disciple there does not perish. Jesus did not say to him that he does not perish but, "If he wishes to remain until I come, what is that to you?"
21:24 This is the disciple who testifies about these things and wrote them, and we know that his testimony is true.
